Solar System

Eris

Eris is a dwarf planet located in the Scattered Disc, a distant region beyond the Kuiper Belt. It is one of the most massive dwarf planets in the Solar System and is slightly smaller than Pluto in diameter. Eris has a rocky and icy surface, one known moon called Dysnomia, and takes about 557 Earth years to orbit the Sun. Its discovery in 2005 led to the reclassification of Pluto as a dwarf planet.

Eris Facts

  • Type:Dwarf Planet
  • Diameter:2,326 km
  • Distance from Sun:10.1 billion km
  • Location:Scattered Disc (Beyond the Kuiper Belt)
  • Day Length:25.9 hours
  • Year Length:558 Earth years
  • Average Temperature:-231°C
  • Gravity:0.82 m/s2
  • Number of Moons:1 (Dysnomia)
  • Atmosphere:Extremely thin, temporary atmosphere of nitrogen (when closest to the Sun); otherwise essentially none
  • Fun Fact:Eris is slightly more massive than Pluto, and its discovery in 2005 led to the reclassification of Pluto as a dwarf planet in 2006.
