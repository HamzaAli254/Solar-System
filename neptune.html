Solar System

neptune

Neptune is the eighth and farthest major planet from the Sun. It is an ice giant with a deep blue color caused by methane in its atmosphere. Neptune is famous for its extremely strong winds, which are the fastest in the Solar System. It has a faint ring system and 16 known moons, with Triton being the largest. A year on Neptune lasts about 165 Earth years, making it the longest planetary year in the Solar System.

Neptune Facts

  • Type:Ice Giant
  • Diameter:49,244 km
  • Distance from Sun:4.50 billion km
  • Day Length:16.1 hours
  • Year Length:164.8 Earth years
  • Average Temperature:-201°C
  • Gravity:11.15 m/s2
  • Number of Moons:16 known moons
  • Atmosphere:Mostly hydrogen and helium, with methane that gives Neptune its deep blue color
  • Fun Fact:Neptune has the fastest winds in the Solar System, reaching speeds of over 2,100 km/h (1,300 mph).
