Oort Cloud

The Oort Cloud is a vast, distant cloud of icy objects that surrounds the Solar System. It is believed to be the outermost region of the Sun's gravitational influence and may extend up to 100,000 astronomical units (AU) from the Sun. Scientists think the Oort Cloud is the source of many long-period comets that travel into the inner Solar System. Although no spacecraft has reached it, its existence is strongly supported by observations of comet orbits.

Oort Cloud Facts

  • Type:Spherical cloud of icy objects
  • Width:3.2 trillion km (20,000 AU)
  • Distance from Sun:300 billion-15 trillion km (2,000-100,000 AU)
  • Year Length:Thousands to millions of Earth years
  • Number of Objects:Billions to trillions of icy bodies
  • Fun Fact:The Oort Cloud is thought to be the source of most long-period comets that enter the inner Solar System.
