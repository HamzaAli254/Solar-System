Solar System

Ceres

Ceres is the largest object in the Asteroid Belt and is classified as a dwarf planet. It lies between the orbits of Mars and Jupiter and is made of rock and ice. Scientists believe Ceres may contain large amounts of frozen water beneath its surface. It was the first object discovered in the Asteroid Belt, in 1801, and takes about 4.6 Earth years to orbit the Sun.

Ceres Facts

  • Type:Dwarf Planet
  • Diameter:939 km
  • Distance from Sun:413.7 million km
  • Location:In the Asteroid Belt, between Mars and Jupiter
  • Day Length:9.1 hours
  • Year Length:4.6 Earth years
  • Average Temperature:-105°C
  • Gravity:0.27 m/s2
  • Number of Moons:0
  • Atmosphere:Extremely thin, temporary exosphere containing water vapor
  • Fun Fact:Ceres is the largest object in the Asteroid Belt and the only dwarf planet located in the inner Solar System.
