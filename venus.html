Solar System

Venus

Venus is the second planet from the Sun and is similar in size to Earth, which is why it is often called Earth's twin. It has a thick atmosphere made mostly of carbon dioxide that traps heat, making Venus the hottest planet in the Solar System. Its surface is covered with volcanoes, mountains, and rocky plains. Venus has no moons or rings, and one day on Venus is longer than its year because it rotates very slowly.

Venus Facts

  • Type:Terrestrial
  • Diameter:12,104 km
  • Distance from Sun:108.2 million km
  • Day Length:243 Earth days
  • Year Length:225 Earth days
  • Average Temperature:464°C
  • Gravity:8.87 m/s2
  • Number of Moons:0
  • Atmosphere:Thick atmosphere of carbon dioxide with clouds of sulfuric acid
  • Fun Fact:Venus rotates backward (retrograde rotation), so the Sun rises in the west and sets in the east.
