Solar System

Asteroid Belt

The Asteroid Belt is a region of space located between the orbits of Mars and Jupiter. It contains millions of rocky and metallic objects called asteroids, ranging in size from tiny rocks to dwarf planets like Ceres. These objects are leftovers from the early formation of the Solar System that never combined to form a planet. Although it contains many asteroids, they are spread far apart, so collisions are relatively rare.

Belt Facts

  • Type:Region of rocky and metallic objects
  • width:140 million km
  • Distance from Sun:329-478 million km
  • Year Length:3-6 Earth years
  • Number of Asteroids:Millions, including over 1 million larger than 1 km
  • Fun Fact:Although it contains millions of asteroids, the Asteroid Belt is so spread out that spacecraft can pass through it with very little chance of hitting an asteroid.
