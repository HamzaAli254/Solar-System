Solar System

Mars

Mars is the fourth planet from the Sun and is known as the Red Planet because of the iron oxide, or rust, on its surface. It is a rocky planet with mountains, valleys, canyons, and polar ice caps. Mars has a thin atmosphere made mostly of carbon dioxide and has two small moons, Phobos and Deimos. A year on Mars lasts 687 Earth days, and scientists continue to explore it in the search for signs of past life.

Mars Facts

  • Type:Terrestrial
  • Diameter:6,779 km
  • Distance from Sun:227.9 million km
  • Day Length:24.6 hours
  • Year Length:687 Earth days
  • Average Temperature:-63°C
  • Gravity:3.71 m/s2
  • Number of Moons:2 (Phobos and Deimos)
  • Atmosphere:Thin atmosphere made mostly of carbon dioxide, with nitrogen and argon
  • Fun Fact:Mars is home to Olympus Mons, the largest volcano and tallest mountain in the Solar System.
