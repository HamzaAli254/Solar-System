Solar System

Scattered Disc

The Scattered Disc is a distant region of the Solar System located beyond the Kuiper Belt. It contains icy objects with highly stretched and tilted orbits, likely caused by the gravitational influence of Neptune. This region is home to dwarf planets such as Eris and many other distant objects. The Scattered Disc extends hundreds of astronomical units (AU) from the Sun and is considered one of the most remote parts of the Solar System.

Scattered Disc Facts

  • Type:Region of distant icy objects
  • Width:75 billion km (extends roughly from 30 AU to over 500 AU)
  • Distance from Sun:4.5-75+ billion km (30 AU-500+ AU)
  • Year Length:Hundreds to thousands of Earth years
  • Number of Objects:Thousands of known objects; likely millions exist
  • Fun Fact:The Scattered Disc is home to Eris, one of the largest dwarf planets, and many of its objects have highly elongated orbits.
