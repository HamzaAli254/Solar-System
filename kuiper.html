Solar System

Kuiper Belt

The Kuiper Belt is a large region of icy objects beyond the orbit of Neptune. It is home to many dwarf planets, including Pluto, Haumea, Makemake, and many smaller icy bodies. Scientists believe the Kuiper Belt contains leftovers from the formation of the Solar System over 4.5 billion years ago. It stretches from about 30 to 55 astronomical units (AU) from the Sun and is the source of many short-period comets.

Kuiper Belt Facts

  • Type:Region of icy objects
  • Width:7 billion km
  • Distance from Sun:4.5-8.2 billion km (30-55 AU)
  • Year Length:200-500 Earth years
  • Number of Objects:Millions of icy bodies
  • Fun Fact:The Kuiper Belt is home to several dwarf planets, including Pluto, Haumea, and Makemake, and is the source of many short-period comets.
