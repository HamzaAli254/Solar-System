Solar System

Uranus

Uranus is the seventh planet from the Sun and is known as an ice giant. It has a blue-green color because of methane gas in its atmosphere. Unlike most planets, Uranus rotates on its side, giving it very unusual seasons that last for many years. It has a faint ring system and 28 known moons. A year on Uranus lasts about 84 Earth years, making it one of the slowest planets to orbit the Sun.

Uranus Facts

  • Type:Ice Giant
  • Diameter:50,724 km
  • Distance from Sun:2.87 billion km
  • Day Length:17.2 hours
  • Year Length:84 Earth years
  • Average Temperature:-195°C
  • Gravity:8.69 m/s2
  • Number of Moons:28 known moons
  • Atmosphere:Mostly hydrogen and helium, with methane that gives the planet its blue-green color
  • Fun Fact:Uranus rotates on its side with an axial tilt of about 98°, making it the most tilted planet in the Solar System.
