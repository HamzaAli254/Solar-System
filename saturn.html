Saturn

Saturn is the sixth planet from the Sun and is best known for its beautiful system of bright rings made of ice and rock. It is a gas giant composed mainly of hydrogen and helium. Saturn has more than 140 known moons, including Titan, the second-largest moon in the Solar System. Despite its enormous size, Saturn is the least dense planet and could float in water if there were a body of water large enough. A year on Saturn lasts about 29.5 Earth years.

Saturn Facts

  • Type:Gas Giant
  • Diameter:116,460 km
  • Distance from Sun:1.43 billion km
  • Day Length:10.7 hours
  • Year Length:29.46 Earth years
  • Average Temperature:-178°C
  • Gravity:10.44 m/s2
  • Number of Moons:274 known moons
  • Atmosphere:Mostly hydrogen and helium, with traces of methane and ammonia
  • Fun Fact:Saturn's spectacular ring system is made mostly of ice particles mixed with rock and dust, and it is the most extensive ring system of any planet in the Solar System.
