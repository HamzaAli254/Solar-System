Makemake

Makemake is a dwarf planet located in the Kuiper Belt beyond Neptune. It is one of the largest known objects in this distant region and has a surface covered with frozen methane, ethane, and nitrogen. Makemake has one known moon, discovered in 2016. It takes about 305 Earth years to complete one orbit around the Sun and was officially recognized as a dwarf planet in 2008.

Makemake Facts

  • Type:Dwarf Planet
  • Diameter:1,434 km
  • Distance from Sun:6.85 billion km
  • Location:Kuiper Belt (Beyond Neptune)
  • Day Length:22.8 hours
  • Year Length:305 Earth years
  • Average Temperature:-239°C
  • Gravity:0.5 m/s2
  • Number of Moons:1 (MK 2)
  • Atmosphere:Very thin, temporary atmosphere of nitrogen and methane (likely present only near perihelion)
  • Fun Fact:Makemake was discovered in 2005 and is named after the creator deity of the Rapa Nui (Easter Island) people.
