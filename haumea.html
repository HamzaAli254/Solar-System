Solar System

Haumea

Haumea is a dwarf planet located in the Kuiper Belt beyond Neptune. It has an unusual elongated shape because it spins very quickly, completing one rotation in about 4 hours. Haumea is made mostly of rock and ice and has a thin ring system. It has two known moons, Hiʻiaka and Namaka, and takes about 285 Earth years to orbit the Sun.

Haumea Facts

  • Type:Dwarf Planet
  • Diameter:1,620 km
  • Distance from Sun:6.45 billion km
  • Location:Kuiper Belt
  • Day Length:3.9 hours
  • Year Length:285 Earth years
  • Average Temperature:-241°C
  • Gravity:0.44 m/s2
  • Number of Moons:2 (Hi'iaka and Namaka)
  • Atmosphere:None known
  • Fun Fact:Haumea is one of the fastest-spinning large objects in the Solar System. Its rapid rotation stretches it into an oval (football-like) shape, making it one of the most unusual dwarf planets.
