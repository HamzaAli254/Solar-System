Solar System

Pluto

Pluto is a dwarf planet located in the Kuiper Belt, a region beyond Neptune filled with icy objects. Once considered the ninth planet, Pluto was reclassified as a dwarf planet in 2006. It has a rocky and icy surface, a thin atmosphere, and five known moons, with Charon being the largest. Pluto takes about 248 Earth years to complete one orbit around the Sun.

Pluto Facts

  • Type:Dwarf Planet
  • Diameter:2,377 km
  • Distance from Sun:5.91 billion km
  • Location:Kuiper Belt(beyond Neptune)
  • Day Length:6.39 Earth days
  • Year Length:248 Earth years
  • Average Temperature:-229°C
  • Gravity:0.62 m/s2
  • Number of Moons:5
  • Atmosphere:Thin atmosphere of nitrogen, methane, and carbon monoxide
  • Fun Fact:Pluto has a giant heart-shaped region called Tombaugh Regio, one of the most recognizable features in the Solar System.
